Earth

Earth, our home planet, is the only world in the universe known to support life. Its unique combination of liquid water, an oxygen-rich atmosphere, and a protective magnetic field makes it habitable. About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by oceans, and its atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace gases like carbon dioxide.

Earth’s interior is dynamic, with a molten core that generates its magnetic field, protecting us from harmful solar radiation. Plate tectonics shape its surface, creating mountains, valleys, and earthquakes.

Formation and Structure of Earth

Earth formed in the Sun’s habitable zone, allowing liquid water to exist. Its differentiated structure includes:

  • Core: Iron and nickel, generating a protective magnetic field.
  • Mantle: Silicate materials driving plate tectonics.
  • Crust: Diverse terrains, from oceans to mountains.

Significance

Earth’s dynamic geology and favorable position support life, making it a unique gem in the Solar System.

Fun Facts

  • Earth is not a perfect sphere; it’s slightly flattened at the poles and bulges at the equator.
  • The planet’s rotation is gradually slowing down by about 1.7 milliseconds per century.
  • Earth’s moon is the fifth-largest natural satellite in the Solar System and plays a crucial role in stabilizing the planet’s tilt and climate.
